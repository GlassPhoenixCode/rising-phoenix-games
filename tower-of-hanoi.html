⚜  Scrolls of the Tower — Rules & How to Play ▾

Legend tells of an ancient temple where monks were set a divine task: to move a tower of sacred golden discs from one pillar to another, one disc at a time, never placing a larger disc upon a smaller. Only when the tower is fully rebuilt on the far peg will their vigil be complete — and the world, they say, shall end when they finish. Choose your disc count wisely.

🎯

The Objective

Move the entire stack of discs from Peg I to Peg III, rebuilding the tower in the same order — largest at the bottom, smallest at the top.

☝️

One Disc at a Time

On each turn you may lift only the topmost disc from any peg and place it onto another peg. You cannot move a buried disc without first moving whatever rests upon it.

⚖️

The Sacred Law

A larger disc may never be placed on top of a smaller one. This is the one unbreakable rule. Peg II is your free middle ground — use it as a staging pillar.

🖱️

How to Move

Click a peg to lift its top disc — it will rise to show it is selected. Then click the destination peg to place it. Click the same peg again to cancel the lift.

✦

Hints & Undo

Tap Hint to have the optimal next move revealed. Tap Undo to reverse your last move. Neither counts against you — this is a game of patience, not punishment.

⚡

Auto-Solve

Press Auto-Solve to watch the optimal solution play out in real time. The game resets first, then performs the minimum required moves — a perfect demonstration of the algorithm.

⚜  Optimal Move Counts by Difficulty

3 Discs — ApprenticeCan be solved in exactly 7 moves. A fine introduction to the rhythm of the puzzle.
4 Discs — Journeyman15 moves required. The pattern begins to reveal itself across two full cycles.
5 Discs — Adept31 moves. The default challenge — long enough to require planning, short enough to finish.
6 Discs — Master63 moves. Patience and a clear mental model of the recursive structure are rewarded.
7 Discs — Archmaster127 moves. A serious undertaking. Each mistake compounds across subsequent cycles.
8 Discs — Legendary255 moves. The full ritual. Even at optimal, this is a test of sustained concentration.

The formula is always 2ⁿ − 1, where n is the number of discs. Your move count and best score for each disc count are saved automatically.
