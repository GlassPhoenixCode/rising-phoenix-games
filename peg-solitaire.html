Strategy Notes
Classic English 33-hole cross board

How to Play

  1. Click or tap a peg to select it.
  2. Choose an empty socket exactly two spaces away horizontally or vertically.
  3. The selected peg leaps into that socket, and the jumped peg is removed.
  4. Diagonal moves are never allowed.
  5. You win when only one peg remains. The classic ideal finish leaves that peg in the center.

Winning Language

A complete win is one peg left anywhere on the board. A perfect classic win is one peg left in the middle. If the board still has pegs but no legal jumps remain, the round is over and you are stalled.

Practical Heuristics

Work inward rather than scattering survivors along the arms of the cross. Preserve mobility near the center, avoid isolating corner-arm pegs too early, and use Undo liberally when a sequence collapses your future options.
